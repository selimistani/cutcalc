Answers

Who Created This Calculator and Why?

Hey, I'm Selim. I've been into bodybuilding since I was 12 years old and have been finding stuff to make or calculate to procrastinate actually working out ever since then. Hence, why this was created, along with being my final project for the CS50x program :).

What is the Purpose of This Calculator?

The purpose of this calculator is to help you set a plan for a cut while losing as little muscle as possible. It assumes that you should lose your BFP% / 20 percent of your body weight, which is based on a study that I admittedly forgot (but you can trust me, I did sports science in IB).

How Accurate is the Body Fat Percentage (BFP) Measurement?

It depends on how well your eyes work. If you think you’re overestimating, add 2-3% to whatever number you come up with.

How Do I Know Which Activity Level to Choose?

If you're unsure, pick an activity level that feels closest to your typical day. If you’re overly optimistic about how active you are, you might end up overestimating how many calories you burn.

What Happens if I Don’t Know My Body Fat Percentage?

If you don’t know your BFP, the calculator will use a different method to estimate your maintenance calories. It’s less accurate, but it’s better than guessing blindly.

Can I Trust the Results from This Calculator?

Take everything with a grain of salt. TL;DR: These are estimates, and while they’re based on science, your body isn’t a simple math equation.

Why Do the Calorie Recommendations Seem Different from Other Calculators?

The other ones suck. Kidding, but really, this calculator uses the Katch-McArdle equation, which is a bit more precise—especially if you know your body fat percentage.

Can I Manually Adjust the Values if I Think They’re Wrong?

I’m too lazy to code this in (I admit it). But feel free to tweak the numbers in your head if you think they’re off.

How Should I Use the Results from This Calculator?

Use an app like MyFitnessPal to track your calories. If your weight loss seems way off after two weeks, adjust accordingly. Keep in mind that the first two weeks can be unreliable as you lose water weight and stuff.

What Are the Limitations of This Calculator?

This calculator provides estimates, not guarantees. It assumes everyone is a bit average, which we both know isn't true, so consider your unique factors when using it.

Why Do I Need to Know My Lean Body Mass (LBM)?

Your LBM helps to calculate how many calories your body burns at rest, which is crucial for estimating your maintenance calories more accurately.

How Often Should I Update My Data in the Calculator?

Once is enough, really. Your LBM and BFP% don’t change drastically overnight, so don’t stress about updating constantly.

Is It Safe to Follow the Calorie Recommendations Provided?

It should be, but always consult a health professional before making any drastic changes to your diet or exercise routine.

What If I Lose Weight Too Quickly or Too Slowly?

If your weight loss is off after two weeks, start worrying. The first two weeks can be unpredictable due to water weight fluctuations, so give it some time before you make adjustments.

Why Does the Activity Level Section Mention Outdated Research?

We have no other research to go on, but it’s optional and still a decent rule of thumb (ish). Consider it a starting point rather than gospel.

Can I Use This Calculator for Gaining Weight or Building Muscle?

Not sure how you would for now, but I plan on adding this functionality later after I learn proper UX / web design. Stay tuned!

Why Do I Need to Trust the Charts for BFP Estimation?

The charts are there to give you a rough idea. No method outside of advanced medical imaging is going to be 100% accurate, so use them as a general guide rather than an absolute measure.

How Do I Measure My Height and Weight Accurately for the Best Results?

Measure your height and weight in the morning, naked (I know, eewwww), and after using the restroom. This gives you the most consistent baseline.

What Should I Do if I Don’t See Any Progress?

If you’re not seeing progress, revisit your inputs and consider adjusting your calorie intake or activity level. Sometimes, it’s just a matter of tweaking the numbers and being patient.

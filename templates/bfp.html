So, You Want to Measure Your Body Fat Percentage?

Let's get one thing straight: getting an exact measurement of your body fat percentage is like trying to catch a greased pig at a county fair. Unless you're willing to hop into an MRI machine (and trust me, if a hospital lets you use an MRI for something this trivial, you should probably find another hospital), you're not going to get a truly accurate number. But that's okay! We’re not here for precision down to the last decimal point. Instead, let's keep things simple with a good old chart to get a ballpark figure. Remember that everybody has different fat distribution, so don't only look at the midsection but consider every part of your body from your arms to your legs.

Below, you'll find a chart that will help you estimate your body fat percentage based on visual cues. It’s not rocket science, but it’s good enough for our purposes.

Remember, this chart is just an estimate. Your body is unique, and numbers can only tell you so much. So, use this visual as a guide, and don’t stress about hitting a specific percentage. It’s all about getting a general idea so you can make informed decisions on your fitness journey.
